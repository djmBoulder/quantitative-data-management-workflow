# Badge Competency Matrix

## Badge Definition

The IBS CU Boulder **Quantitative Data Management and Workflow for Social Scientists** microcredential certifies that a student can manage, document, clean, combine, validate, and export social science data using a reproducible workflow in Stata, R, or both.

The badge is designed for graduate students in the social sciences who need practical preparation for quantitative methods coursework, research assistantships, thesis or dissertation projects, and collaborative data work.

Badge evidence must use synthetic or publicly distributable teaching data only. Students should not submit private, restricted, licensed, confidential, or student data.

## Pathway Options

Students may complete badge evidence through one of three pathways:

- **Stata pathway:** the student submits a reproducible Stata do-file and Stata-generated evidence.
- **R pathway:** the student submits a reproducible R script and R-generated evidence.
- **Dual Stata/R pathway:** the student submits both Stata and R workflows that implement the same major data decisions.

All students should understand both Stata and R workflows conceptually. Unless a specific offering requires both, badge evidence is required in one primary pathway. The dual pathway is recommended for students who want extra practice or expect to move between research teams using different software.

## Minimum Completion Requirements

To earn the badge, students should complete:

1. Orientation and pathway selection.
2. Module assignments or equivalent instructor-approved evidence.
3. A capstone data management portfolio.
4. A reproducible primary-pathway script that runs from the repository root.
5. An analysis-ready dataset generated by the script.
6. A data dictionary or codebook.
7. A QA checklist or QA report.
8. A workflow memo explaining major data decisions.
9. Evidence that appends, merges, missing data, variable coding, and sample restrictions were checked.

The recommended badge standard is **complete/incomplete with revision allowed**. Students earn the badge when the submitted evidence meets the minimum threshold for each competency area below.

## What Counts As Satisfactory Competency Evidence

Satisfactory evidence should show that the student can perform the workflow, document the decision, and explain the result. Evidence is satisfactory when:

- scripts run from the repository root or clearly document required setup,
- relative paths are used,
- raw synthetic data are not manually edited,
- generated files can be recreated from code,
- key cleaning and recoding decisions are documented,
- missing data and sample restrictions are explicit,
- appends and merges include row-count and key diagnostics,
- QA checks distinguish fatal errors from warnings,
- the data dictionary or codebook is understandable,
- and the workflow memo explains major decisions in plain language.

## What Requires Revision Before Badge Completion

A submission should be returned for revision if any of the following are true:

- The script does not run and the issue is not clearly documented.
- Raw or synthetic source files were manually edited.
- The workflow uses private, restricted, licensed, confidential, or student data.
- Absolute paths make the workflow specific to one computer.
- Required source files, outputs, or documentation are missing.
- Cleaning or recoding decisions are not documented.
- Special missing codes are treated as valid values without explanation.
- Observations are dropped without an analytic sample flag or sample restriction note.
- Appends or merges are performed without key, duplicate, unmatched-record, or row-count checks.
- QA evidence is missing or only states that checks were done without showing results.
- The data dictionary, codebook, or workflow memo is too vague for another researcher to understand the workflow.
- Generated working/output/log files are committed to GitHub when repository policy says they should remain ignored.

## Optional Advanced Or Dual-Language Extension Evidence

Students seeking additional practice may submit extension evidence. Instructors may also require some of these items in a specific offering.

Optional evidence includes:

- parallel Stata and R scripts that implement the same capstone workflow,
- a short comparison memo explaining differences between the Stata and R workflows,
- additional QA checks beyond the minimum requirement,
- an expanded codebook with source variables, derived variables, valid values, and known limitations,
- a reproducible summary table package for advisor or collaborator review,
- a peer-review response memo describing revisions made after feedback,
- or a short walkthrough showing how to rerun the workflow from a clean project folder.

Dual-language evidence should be evaluated for substantive parallelism. The Stata and R workflows do not need to be line-by-line identical, but they should use the same source data, implement the same major decisions, and produce comparable final outputs.

## Policy Decisions For Instructor Or IBS Review

Before launch, instructors or IBS leadership should confirm:

- whether module assignments are required badge evidence or formative preparation for the capstone,
- whether the capstone alone can satisfy summative badge evidence,
- whether any offering requires dual Stata/R submission,
- the exact number of revision attempts and resubmission window,
- where students should submit generated outputs that should not be committed to GitHub,
- whether peer review is required,
- whether the badge is complete/incomplete only or uses performance levels,
- and whether local policy language is needed for accessibility, academic integrity, data security, or noncredit transcript/badge records.
