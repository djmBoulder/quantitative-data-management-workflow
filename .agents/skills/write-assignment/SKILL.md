# Write Assignment Skill

Use this skill when drafting or revising a student-facing `assignment.md`.

## Goals

- Give students a clear task that practices the module skill.
- Keep instructions beginner-friendly and action-oriented.
- Use synthetic or publicly distributable data only.
- Ask for evidence of reproducible work, not just final answers.
- Keep instructor answers and grading notes out of the assignment.

## Assignment Pattern

Include:

- Purpose: why the task matters.
- Data: which synthetic files students should use.
- Tasks: numbered steps students should complete.
- Expected files or outputs.
- Reflection or documentation prompt when useful.
- Submission checklist.
- Reminder not to submit generated data unless requested.

## Student-Facing Rules

- Do not reveal solution notes, expected values, answer keys, or grading rubrics.
- Ask students to use relative paths.
- Ask students to protect raw data.
- Include common mistakes or debugging hints, but not final answers.
- Make Stata and R expectations parallel when both are required.

## Before Finishing

- Confirm the assignment matches the module lesson and labs.
- Confirm the task can be completed with synthetic data.
- Confirm required outputs are realistic for beginners.
- Confirm the checklist tells students exactly what to submit.
